# THE 72 PENTADS: FUNDAMENTAL ARCHITECTURE
## What is a pentad?

### Rowlands' Definition

A **pentad** is a fundamental mathematical structure that combines the four universal physical parameters:

| Parameter | Notation |
|-----------|----------|
| Time | $i'$ (pseudoscalar) |
| Space | $\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{k}$ |
| Mass | $1$ |
| Charge | $\mathbf{I}, \mathbf{J}, \mathbf{K}$ |

### The construction of a pentad

According to Rowlands, a pentad is constructed by taking the components of a conserved parameter (charge) and superimposing them on the units of the other three parameters:

1.  **Starting point**: The four fundamental parameters:
    - Time: i' (pseudoscalar)
    - Space: i, j, k (vectors)
    - Mass: 1 (scalar)
    - Charge: I, J, K (vectors)

2.  **Application**: Each unit of charge $(I, J, K)$ is applied to the expressions for time $(i')$, space $(i, j, k)$ and mass $(1)$.

3.  **Result**: A pentad of 5 composite elements.

### Concrete example (Family I)

Taking the charge units $(I, J, K)$ and applying them:

| Step | Operation | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Charge $K$ on Time $i'$ | $Ki'$ |
| 2 | Charge $I$ on Space $i, j, k$ | $Ii, Ij, Ik$ |
| 3 | Charge $J$ on Mass $1$ | $1J$ |

This gives the pentad: $\{Ki', Ii, Ij, Ik, 1J\}$

### Why 5 elements?

The pentad contains exactly:
- **3 structural elements** (space-charge: $Ii, Ij, Ik$)
- **1 transformation** (time-charge: $Ki'$)
- **1 substance** (mass-charge: $1J$)

More generally, this involves combining two one-dimensional quantities with two three-dimensional quantities. The process can be applied to other ontologies (economics, phonology, chemistry, etc.). This 3-1-1 architecture is universal and corresponds to the five elements of the Wu Xing.
